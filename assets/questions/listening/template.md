# TOEFL Listening Practice - TPO 01

## Module 1

### Listen to a Conversation – Questions 1-5
Listen to a conversation between a student and a university librarian.

audio: conversation1.mp3

The student is looking for resources about environmental science for a research paper. The librarian suggests several databases and mentions a new book that just arrived.

1. What is the student's main purpose for visiting the library?
A. To return overdue books
B. To find research materials for a paper
C. To apply for a library card
D. To attend a workshop on database usage

2. What does the librarian recommend as the best resource?
A. The general encyclopedia
B. The science fiction section
C. The online academic databases
D. The newspaper archives

3. Why does the librarian mention the new book?
A. It is written by a famous professor
B. It covers the exact topic the student needs
C. It has colorful illustrations
D. It is available as an e-book only

4. What does the student need to do to access the databases from home?
A. Pay a subscription fee
B. Use the university VPN
C. Visit the library in person
D. Get special permission from a professor

5. What will the student probably do next?
A. Go to the science fiction section
B. Check out the new book
C. Search the online databases
D. Attend a library workshop

### Listen to an Academic Talk – Questions 6-10
Listen to part of a lecture in a biology class.

audio: lecture1.mp3

The professor is discussing the concept of symbiosis in ecological relationships, focusing on mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism.

6. What is the main topic of the lecture?
A. The history of biology
B. Types of symbiotic relationships
C. Animal behavior patterns
D. Plant photosynthesis

7. According to the professor, what characterizes mutualism?
A. One organism benefits, the other is unaffected
B. Both organisms benefit
C. One organism benefits, the other is harmed
D. Both organisms are harmed

8. What example does the professor give for commensalism?
A. Bees and flowers
B. Tapeworms and humans
C. Barnacles and whales
D. Clownfish and sea anemones

9. How does the professor describe parasitism?
A. A rare phenomenon in nature
B. A relationship where both parties benefit
C. A temporary arrangement
D. A relationship where one benefits at the other's expense

10. What does the professor imply about the importance of understanding symbiosis?
A. It is only relevant for biology students
B. It helps explain many ecological interactions
C. It is a controversial theory
D. It has limited real-world applications
